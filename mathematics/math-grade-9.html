Mathematics - Grade 9

Algebra 1 introduces students to the fundamental concepts of algebraic expressions, equations, and functions. Key topics include solving linear

equations and inequalities, graphing linear functions, understanding systems of equations, and an introduction to quadratic functions and polynomials.

The course emphasizes developing problem-solving strategies, logical reasoning, and the application of algebraic principles to real-world situations,

building a strong foundation for future mathematics courses.

View Algebra I Curriculum

Geometry provides a comprehensive exploration of fundamental geometric concepts. Students will delve into Euclidean geometry, studying properties of points, lines, planes, and solids. Key topics include logical reasoning and proofs, transformations (translations, rotations, reflections, dilations), congruence and similarity of figures, properties of triangles and quadrilaterals, circles, and an introduction to trigonometry. The course emphasizes problem-solving skills, critical thinking, and the application of geometric principles to real-world scenarios, preparing students for higher-level mathematics.

This Math Proficiency course is designed to strengthen students' foundational mathematical skills and concepts essential for success in higher-level mathematics and various real-world applications. The curriculum focuses on reinforcing arithmetic operations, number sense, pre-algebraic concepts, problem-solving strategies, and data interpretation. Through targeted practice and conceptual understanding, students will build confidence and develop the necessary proficiency to tackle more advanced mathematical challenges.

View Math Proficiency Curriculum

This Algebra 2 course expands upon the concepts introduced in Algebra 1, delving deeper into advanced algebraic topics. Students will explore polynomial, rational, exponential, and logarithmic functions, as well as sequences and series, probability, and statistics. The curriculum emphasizes complex problem-solving, analytical thinking, and the application of advanced algebraic techniques to real-world scenarios, preparing students for pre-calculus and other higher-level mathematics.

This Personal Finance course equips students with essential knowledge and skills to manage their financial lives effectively. Topics covered include budgeting, saving, investing basics, understanding credit and debt, banking, insurance, and long-term financial planning. The course emphasizes practical applications and decision-making, empowering students to make informed choices for their financial well-being and future.
